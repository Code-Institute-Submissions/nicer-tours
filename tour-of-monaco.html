Monaco! Monte-Carlo!

The names themselves are enough to conjure up images of wealth, prosperity and glamour. Per capita, the richest country in the world. Home to 122 different nationalities. Nobles, movie stars and sporting heroes all choose to make Monaco their home.

An independent country since 1297, the country was founded on the rock on which the Palais Princier today stands. The second smallest country in the world, occupying just 2 square kilometres of land, Monaco has always been a draw for the elite.

Famed today for its Casino, its luxurious hotels and finery, Monaco never disappoints. Unless you’re one of the unlucky ones who puts everything on red and sees your token land on black…

Before arriving in Monaco itself, you’ll take a detour to the village of Eze, perched up high above Monaco and once on its very border.

Features of this tour:

The village of Eze

Be sure to bring good walking shoes, as the village streets are cobbled, and the climb up is steep. Dominating the surrounding area, the village was founded during the time of the Egyptian pharaohs. Indeed, its takes its very name from the Egyptian god of beauty, Isis. The village became a stronghold during the time of the invasions of the Saracens, as the local inhabitants moved to the high hills for safety. Defence has always been a watchword for the village - it influences the width of the streets, the design of the village entrance and its very position.

A photo opportunity from which you can photograph an entire country*

Above Monaco is the town of La Turbie, from which you will find a stunning view over the whole of the principality. The photo opportunity itself is down a path made from broken stones and then from the limestone blocks that make up the Tête de Chien mountain. Again, be sure to wear good walking shoes!



*Note that in inclement weather, the cloud is often lower than the viewpoint, so during days of bad weather, this stop will not be part of the tour.

Monaco town and the Rock

You’ll wind your way into Monaco along the road upon which Princess Grace, Grace Kelly, lost her life in 1982. A sombre note that allows you to appreciate the Princes' Palace all the more and tower that housed Grace’s office. From here, you’ll appreciate how Grace influenced the destiny of a country, bringing as she did the esteem and glamour for which it has since become so famous. Choosing the 8 hour option for this tour, you’ll have time to lunch at leisure in the shadow of the palace, and even visit its State Rooms and Throne Room*.



*Admission to the palace not included in the price of the tour.

The Grand Prix Racetrack

Famous for its Formula One race, Monaco has held car races since 1929. You’ll take a drive around the track, as the race is held on public roads. Soundtrack not included, but you’re welcome to imitate the screeches of the tyres as you’re driven along the track!

Casino Square

Inaugurated in 1863, the casino in Monaco was not the world’s first casino, but it is certainly the world’s most famous. Classically beautiful architecture, the beauty is carried over into the games rooms, both those open to the public and those reserved for the private games. If you’ve a few bucks to spare, you can either lose them all on the red, or peruse the luxury boutiques where the winners spend their gains!
