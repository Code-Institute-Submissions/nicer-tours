Your Tour Of Nice:

Queen of the Riviera. City of light and life.

Founded by the Phoenicians and named for the Greek goddess of victory, Nike, the original name of Nice was Nikaïa.

Passing between the hands of the French, the Counts of Savoy and the House of Genova, the city has always enjoyed a privileged position on the Mediterranean coast and was clearly always a prize to treasure.

As still it is!

Famed today for its Flower Market, its food and its laidback lifestyle, Nice is a prime destination for any short or long break. Boasting 300 days of sun a year, your tour is virtually guaranteed to take place under the blue skies of this glorious location!

Features of this tour:

The old town and its famous flower market and traditional "marché provençal”:

Enjoy the atmosphere of this typically provencal market square. Take time to admire the beauty of the blooms coming from all over the world, some destined for the perfume industry (the second biggest industry of the area, after tourism), some for the villas, townhouses and apartments that line the Promenade…

Castle Hill with its breathtaking view of the Bay of Angels:

You’ll be taken up to admire the beauty of the Promenade des Anglais, named for the English aristocrats who financed its construction. The perfect photo opportunity - as you can see from the banner image that heads this very page!

A stroll along the port, to admire the yachts:

Take a stroll along either quayside of Port Lympia. The man-made port basin was once at the mouth of the Paillon river. Now diverted to run underneath the city, its estuary formed the perfect basin for the port, construction of which started in 1750. Always destined to be a pleasure port, as Nice has not been a commercial city since the time of the Phoenicians, the port today is home to some of the finest motor and sailing yachts in the world.

The Russian Orthodox Cathedral:

The largest Russian Orthodox Cathedral outside of Russia, this magnificent House of God was built under the watchful eye of Tsar Nicholas II, frequent visitor to Nice. Its garden houses a shrine to the son of the Tsar, Nicholas Alexandroich, who died of meningitis while in Nice. Since 2011, after a long legal dispute, the cathedral has been the property of the Russian Federation. Effectively, it's a little corner of Russia in the South of France. But don’t worry: you don’t need to bring your passports to visit the cathedral!

The Cimiez gardens and monastery:

Site of the Roman city of Cemenelum and its gladiatorial arena, the gardens today are a grove of olive trees and boulevards named after the Jazz icons who once played at the Nice Jazz Festival. The park still seems to echo with the music of these festivals! Nestled in a corner of the park is the Matisse Museum which houses a large collection of the artist’s works. Matisse himself was a resident of Nice from 1917 until 1954. At the far end of the park you’ll find the 16th century Franciscan monastery. Be sure to visit Matisse’s tomb in the cemetery.
